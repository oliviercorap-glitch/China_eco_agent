Executive summary
China's economy is showing a fragile but positive recovery, with Q1 2026 GDP growing 5.0% and the manufacturing PMI returning to expansion at 50.1. The government has set a 4.5-5% growth target for 2026, the first below 5% in 30 years, signaling a realistic assessment of structural headwinds. The PBOC is maintaining a moderately loose monetary policy with further rate cuts expected, which supports both TLD's financing costs and customer capex. A major 4800 billion yuan aviation infrastructure investment plan is confirmed, creating a strong pipeline for GSE demand. However, a critical lithium price surge of 150% from 60,000 to 150,000 yuan per tonne is directly impacting electric GSE battery costs and margins. Steel prices remain benign with 3-4% YoY increases, while aluminium is entering a boom cycle with surging overseas orders. The US-China trade deal rolling back 30 billion dollars in tariffs is a positive development for trade stability and component costs. The helium export ban and new data compliance regulations add operational complexity. The net picture is one of strong demand tailwinds from infrastructure investment offset by significant input cost pressure from lithium and aluminium, requiring active margin management.
