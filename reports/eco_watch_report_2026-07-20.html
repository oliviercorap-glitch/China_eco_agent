Executive summary
China's Q2 GDP miss at 4.3% versus the 5% target signals broad economic weakness that undermines business confidence and poses a downside risk to GSE orders in H2 2026. However, favorable input cost trends provide a significant offset: steel prices are down 10-15% YoY, aluminium down 5-8%, and lithium collapsed over 70% from peaks, directly improving TLD's manufacturing margins by an estimated 3-5% for steel-intensive products and 5-8% for electric GSE. Demand-side signals are mixed but net positive: 30+ airport projects in the pipeline represent 1,500-2,400 GSE units over 3-5 years, domestic air traffic is up 15% YoY, and the C919 international debut opens new route-driven GSE demand. The CNY has depreciated 2-3% against the EUR, boosting TLD's export competitiveness. The main risk is deteriorating macro conditions including potential US-China tariff escalation and geopolitical tensions in the Taiwan Strait that could disrupt supply chains. Priority actions include locking in favorable steel prices, renegotiating battery supply contracts, and engaging airport expansion projects at Beijing Daxing and Shanghai Pudong.
📉 Manufacturing margin outlook (30-90 days)
Net manufacturing margins for TLD China are expected to improve 3-5% over the next 30-90 days driven by lower steel, aluminium, and lithium input costs. The favorable raw material environment should persist given property sector weakness and lithium oversupply. The main margin risk is deflationary pressure forcing output price reductions that could partially offset input cost gains.
📈 GSE demand outlook (90-180 days)
GSE demand in China is positive over the next 90-180 days, supported by 8-10% infrastructure investment growth, 30+ airport projects, and 15% air traffic growth. The GDP miss and deteriorating macro risks may delay some procurement decisions, but the structural pipeline remains strong. International route recovery and C919 expansion provide additional upside.

TLD's Shanghai and Wuxi factories face a fragile demand environment for non-GSE production.
GSE demand impact
The GDP miss undermines business confidence and could delay airport infrastructure spending and airline fleet capex, directly reducing GSE procurement from handlers and airports. The 0.7 point gap from the official target is a threshold breach requiring urgent attention.
