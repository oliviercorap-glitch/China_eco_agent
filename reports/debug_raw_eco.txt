Steel prices have declined 2.25% month-on-month to USD 1,169/ton, providing modest COGS relief, but remain 33% higher year-on-year. Real estate distress continues to drag on regional government infrastructure spending, including airport investment. The net impact on TLD China is a mixed picture: stable but not growing manufacturing activity, manageable input costs with steel declining, and cautious GSE demand from domestic customers offset by potential export opportunities.
MARGIN_OUTLOOK: Manufacturing margins are under moderate pressure from elevated input costs, with the PMI input price index remaining high despite the monthly steel price decline. The 2.25% drop in HRC steel provides approximately 0.3-0.4% COGS relief on a blended basis, but this is insufficient to offset the 33% year-on-year steel price increase. Labor costs remain stable with low CPI inflation. Net margin outlook for the next 30-90 days is stable to slightly improving, contingent on steel prices continuing their downward trend.
DEMAND_OUTLOOK: GSE demand for the next 90-180 days is cautious to negative for the domestic market, with the NBS PMI at 50.3 and new orders in contraction at 49.2. Airport investment is constrained by real estate-driven fiscal pressures on local governments. Export demand is more promising, supported by the Caixin PMI at 51.8 and new export orders expansion. The PBOC's liquidity injection may support customer financing for GSE leasing in 3-6 months. Overall, expect flat to slightly declining domestic orders with potential export growth.
WATCH_1: NBS Manufacturing PMI for July 2026 — threshold: below 49.5 would signal renewed contraction and trigger GSE demand caution; above 51 would signal recovery and support capex.
WATCH_2: HRC steel price trend — threshold: a further 5% decline from current USD 1,169/ton would provide meaningful margin relief; a 5% increase would compress margins further.
WATCH_3: Domestic air traffic growth data — threshold: year-on-year growth below 5% would confirm weak consumption-to-travel linkage and signal GSE order deferrals.
MAIN_RISK: The combination of weak domestic demand (PMI new orders at 49.2), real estate-driven fiscal constraints on airport investment, and elevated input costs could lead to a significant GSE order contraction in Q3 2026.
MAIN_OPPORTUNITY: Falling battery costs driven by China's EV industry growth and PBOC liquidity easing create a window to aggressively market electric GSE with competitive total cost of ownership and attractive financing terms.
