Q2 GDP growth at 4.3% misses official target, lowest since 2022

Reading
China’s second-quarter GDP grew 4.3% year-on-year, falling short of the 5% official annual target and marking the weakest reading since the end of 2022. This miss of 0.7 percentage points versus the target signals broad economic weakness and deteriorating business confidence.
Manufacturing impact
Weaker GDP growth implies softening domestic demand, which may reduce factory utilization rates at TLD’s Shanghai and Wuxi plants. Lower overall economic activity could also pressure input costs downward, offering a slight offset to margin compression from weaker volumes.
GSE demand impact
The GDP miss is a major negative signal for GSE demand. Airport operators and ground handlers will likely defer or cancel capex plans as business confidence erodes. Air traffic growth is at risk of slowing, reducing the urgency for fleet refreshes.
Recommended action
CFO should immediately review Q3 and Q4 production forecasts and prepare for a potential 10-15% volume downside scenario. Engage with key APAC customers to assess their capex pipeline for the next 6 months.

Important 🏦 MONETARY

PBOC injects 1.4 trillion yuan via 6-month reverse repo to boost liquidity

Reading
The People’s Bank of China conducted a 1.4 trillion yuan 6-month outright reverse repo operation on July 15, 2026, with 900 billion yuan maturing in the same period, resulting in a net injection of 500 billion yuan. This is a significant liquidity operation aimed at easing financial conditions amid slowing growth.
Manufacturing impact
The liquidity injection will lower interbank rates and may reduce TLD’s own working capital financing costs in China. It also signals PBOC’s commitment to support the real economy, which could stabilize supplier credit conditions in the Yangtze Delta.
GSE demand impact
Cheaper financing for customers supports GSE leasing demand, as airport operators and handlers can access lower-cost capital for equipment purchases. However, the effect is likely muted given the broader GDP weakness and cautious business sentiment.
Recommended action
Treasury team should assess if TLD can refinance any existing CNY-denominated debt at lower rates. Sales team should highlight improved leasing terms to customers in APAC.
