Executive summary
China's economy is showing a mixed picture in mid-2026, with Q1 GDP growing 5.0% at the upper end of the target range, supported by strong industrial output and high-tech manufacturing. However, the manufacturing PMI has edged down to 50.3, services are contracting, and industrial profit growth is slowing. The most critical signal for TLD is the lithium price surge to 164000 RMB/tonne, a 30% move in 30 days that breaches the critical threshold and directly impacts electric GSE margins. Aluminium prices are also rising, adding further cost pressure. On the positive side, the CNY has depreciated 9-10% against the EUR, making TLD's China exports more competitive in Europe, and the PBOC's accommodative monetary policy supports customer financing. The US-China trade deal rolling back 30 billion USD in tariffs provides a more stable trade environment. The 2025 air traffic growth of 10.5% sets a strong baseline for GSE demand, though the slowing industrial profit growth warrants monitoring. Priority actions include activating battery price escalation clauses, locking in lithium inventory, and updating export pricing to capture European market share.
📉 Manufacturing margin outlook (30-90 days)
Manufacturing margins face significant pressure in the next 30-90 days from the lithium price surge, which adds 5-7% to electric GSE COGS, and rising aluminium prices adding 1-2% to structural component costs. The CNY depreciation against the EUR provides a partial offset by improving export revenue in CNY terms, but the net margin outlook is negative unless pricing actions are taken quickly.
📈 GSE demand outlook (90-180 days)
GSE demand outlook for the next 90-180 days is moderately positive, supported by 5.0% GDP growth, record air traffic levels, accommodative monetary policy, and the US-China trade deal. The 10.5% air traffic growth in 2025 provides a strong foundation for fleet refreshment cycles. However, slowing industrial profit growth and the services PMI contraction are headwinds that could delay some customer capex decisions.
