Executive summary
China's manufacturing economy is in a fragile equilibrium with the June NBS PMI at 50.3, barely above expansion, while the Caixin PMI at 51.8 shows stronger export-oriented activity. Industrial output grew 4.5% in May, below trend, and retail sales expanded only 1.4% year-to-date, signaling weak domestic consumption. Steel prices have declined 2.25% month-on-month to USD 1,169/ton, providing modest COGS relief, but remain 33% higher year-on-year. The PBOC injected RMB 500 billion via MLF operations, easing liquidity conditions and supporting customer financing. Real estate distress continues to drag on regional government infrastructure spending, including airport investment. The net impact on TLD China is a mixed picture: stable but not growing manufacturing activity, manageable input costs with steel declining, and cautious GSE demand from domestic customers offset by potential export opportunities.
📉 Manufacturing margin outlook (30-90 days)
Manufacturing margins are under moderate pressure from elevated input costs, with the PMI input price index remaining high despite the monthly steel price decline. The 2.25% drop in HRC steel provides approximately 0.3-0.4% COGS relief on a blended basis, but this is insufficient to offset the 33% year-on-year steel price increase. Labor costs remain stable with low CPI inflation. Net margin outlook for the next 30-90 days is stable to slightly improving, contingent on steel prices continuing their downward trend.
📈 GSE demand outlook (90-180 days)
GSE demand for the next 90-180 days is cautious to negative for the domestic market, with the NBS PMI at 50.3 and new orders in contraction at 49.2. Airport investment is constrained by real estate-driven fiscal pressures on local governments. Export demand is more promising, supported by the Caixin PMI at 51.8 and new export orders expansion. The PBOC's liquidity injection may support customer financing for GSE leasing in 3-6 months. Overall, expect flat to slightly declining domestic orders with potential export growth.

Important 🔩 STEEL

HRC steel at USD 1,169, down 2.25% month-on-month

Reading
Hot-rolled coil steel prices fell to USD 1,169.08 per ton on July 3, 2026, down 2.25% over the past month. However, prices remain 33% higher year-on-year. The decline from recent highs reflects new capacity coming online in China and softening demand.
Manufacturing impact
The 2.25% monthly decline in HRC steel provides modest COGS relief for TLD's steel-intensive GSE products, such as tow tractors and belt loaders. Steel accounts for approximately 15-20% of TLD's raw material costs, so this move reduces input costs by roughly 0.3-0.4% on a blended basis.
GSE demand impact
Lower steel prices may slightly improve margins for TLD's customers who fabricate their own equipment, but the impact on GSE procurement decisions is negligible.
Recommended action
