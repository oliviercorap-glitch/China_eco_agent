The manufacturing PMI has stabilized at 50.3 in June after touching 50.0 in May, indicating borderline expansion, while the critical new export orders sub-index fell to 48.6 signaling weakening external demand. The US-China trade deal offers a USD 30 billion tariff rollback mechanism, but new US overcapacity investigations pose fresh tariff risks. Domestic demand weakness from the ongoing property bust and soft consumption creates headwinds for GSE procurement, while continued airport infrastructure investment provides a partial offset. The phase-out of NEV tax breaks by 2027 signals a maturing EV market that may slow battery cost declines.
