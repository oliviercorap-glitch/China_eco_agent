The economic picture for TLD's China operations in July 2026 is mixed with a critical negative signal from air traffic data. The NBS Manufacturing PMI returned to expansion at 50.3 in June, providing a stable manufacturing backdrop, but the 7.1% year-on-year drop in May air passenger traffic is a severe demand shock that breaches our critical threshold. The net impact is a cautious outlook for China GSE demand with significant margin pressure from raw material costs.

We expect a potential 15-20% decline in China domestic GSE orders over the next 90-180 days. However, the US-China trade deal and aircraft import resumption provide a medium-term positive for GSE demand in 12-18 months. International route growth in Q1 is a bright spot for major hub airports.

NBS PMI returns to expansion at 50.3 in June

Reading
China's official NBS Manufacturing PMI rose to 50.3 in June 2026 from 50.0 in May, beating market expectations of 50.1 and returning above the 50-point expansion threshold. The reading was driven by a rise in the production index to 51.4 and new orders index to 51.2, indicating a pickup in manufacturing activity and demand. However, the small enterprise PMI fell to 48.2, signaling continued weakness in the SME sector.
Manufacturing impact
The return to expansion is a positive signal for TLD's factory operations in Shanghai and Wuxi, suggesting stable production conditions. The rise in the new orders index (51.2) indicates improving demand for manufactured goods, which could support TLD's own order book. However, the small enterprise PMI at 48.2 suggests supply chain partners in the SME segment may face ongoing pressure, potentially causing minor delivery delays.
GSE demand impact
A PMI above 50 supports a stable business environment for GSE customers, but at 50.3 it is not yet at the 52+ threshold that would trigger a clear capex unlock signal. The improvement is modest and does not yet indicate a strong expansion cycle for airport or handler investment. Demand outlook remains cautiously stable rather than accelerating.
Recommended action
Monitor next month's PMI for sustained expansion above 50.5 to confirm recovery trend. No immediate action required.

Reading
Chinese HRC steel prices reached CNY 3,473/t on the Shanghai Futures Exchange in late May 2026, a 19-month high. The current spot price is around CNY 3,305/t as of July 6, 2026, with the USD-denominated price at $1,169/t, down 2.25% over the past month but still 33% higher than a year ago. The price increase is driven by production cuts and steady demand from manufacturing.
Manufacturing impact
This is a significant COGS pressure signal for TLD. Steel is a primary input for GSE manufacturing, and a 33% YoY increase directly impacts production costs. Assuming steel represents approximately 15-20% of TLD's raw material costs, this translates to a roughly 5-7% increase in overall material costs. The recent 2.25% monthly decline offers some relief but the YoY comparison remains severe.
GSE demand impact
Higher steel prices may increase the cost of GSE equipment, potentially pressuring TLD's pricing power or margins. However, the impact on customer demand is indirect and likely muted as GSE prices are typically set in contracts with some lag. The primary risk is to TLD's manufacturing margin rather than demand volume.
Recommended action
CFO should immediately review steel procurement contracts and consider hedging or forward purchasing to lock in current prices. Evaluate passing on cost increases to customers in new Q3 2026 quotes. Flag as a margin risk for the next earnings call.
