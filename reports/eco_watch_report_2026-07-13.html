Executive summary
China's macroeconomic environment for TLD Group in July 2026 presents a mixed but net favorable picture for manufacturing margins and GSE demand. Steel prices are down 10-15% year-on-year and aluminium and lithium prices remain soft, directly reducing TLD's COGS and improving margins on both conventional and electric GSE. Air passenger traffic has exceeded pre-COVID levels with 15% year-on-year growth, and infrastructure investment is up 8-10% with over 30 airport projects in the pipeline, signaling strong GSE demand ahead. The CNY/EUR rate is stable at 7.8-7.9, supporting export competitiveness. However, key risks include potential US tariff escalation on industrial goods, persistent deflation squeezing pricing power, and local government fiscal stress that could delay airport projects. The Caixin Manufacturing PMI at 50.5-51.0 indicates steady but unspectacular conditions. Priority actions include locking in favorable steel and lithium pricing, proactively engaging with airport authorities in western and central China, and conducting tariff scenario analysis for US exports.
📉 Manufacturing margin outlook (30-90 days)
Net manufacturing margins for TLD China are expected to improve over the next 30-90 days, driven by 10-15% lower steel costs and soft aluminium and lithium prices. This should expand gross margins by an estimated 3-5% on steel-intensive GSE products and 2-4% on electric GSE. The main risk is deflationary pressure limiting TLD's ability to maintain pricing.
📈 GSE demand outlook (90-180 days)
GSE demand outlook for the next 90-180 days is positive, supported by 15% air passenger traffic growth and 8-10% cargo traffic growth driving fleet replacement needs. The pipeline of over 30 airport projects represents potential demand for 1,500 to 2,400 GSE units over the medium term. Downside risks include local government fiscal stress and potential US tariff escalation.

Important 🔩 STEEL

Chinese steel prices down 10-15% year-on-year, favorable for TLD margins

Reading
Chinese steel prices have declined approximately 10-15% year-on-year due to persistent oversupply from record production and weak property sector demand. Government output curbs are insufficient to fully balance the market, keeping input costs low.
Manufacturing impact
This is a direct and significant positive for TLD's manufacturing margins. Steel is a key input for GSE production, and a 10-15% year-on-year decline in steel prices should reduce TLD's COGS by an estimated 3-5% depending on steel intensity per unit. This improves gross margin on steel-intensive GSE products like tow tractors and belt loaders.
GSE demand impact
No direct impact on GSE demand. Lower steel prices do not directly stimulate airport or airline capex.
Recommended action
CFO should update Q3 2026 margin forecasts to reflect lower steel input costs. Procurement team should lock in current favorable steel pricing with suppliers for the next 2-3 months.
Important ⚙️ ALUMINIUM

Aluminium and lithium prices soft, improving TLD input cost outlook

Reading
Aluminium prices have softened in Q1 2025 due to ample domestic supply and subdued global demand. Lithium prices remain near cyclical lows after the 2023-2024 collapse, with no near-term recovery catalyst. Both metals benefit from China's overcapacity in refining and processing.
Manufacturing impact
Lower aluminium prices reduce COGS for TLD's GSE products using aluminium components, such as passenger boarding bridges and cargo loaders. Lithium at cyclical lows directly improves the economics of electric GSE battery packs, potentially reducing TLD's electric GSE production costs by 2-4% depending on battery content.
GSE demand impact
Lower lithium prices improve the total cost of ownership for electric GSE, potentially accelerating customer adoption of TLD's electrification portfolio. This is a demand tailwind for electric GSE sales.
Recommended action
Procurement team should negotiate multi-month fixed-price contracts for aluminium and lithium-based battery components to lock in current favorable pricing. Marketing team should highlight lower battery costs in electric GSE sales pitches.

Watch 💱 FX

CNY/EUR stable at 7.8-7.9, gradual depreciation bias supports exports

Reading
The CNY has depreciated modestly against the EUR over the past 6 months, trading around 7.8-7.9 CNY per EUR. This reflects China's monetary easing and Europe's relatively tighter policy. The range is expected to persist with a gradual depreciation bias.
Manufacturing impact
A stable-to-weaker CNY improves TLD's cost competitiveness for exports from China to Europe and other EUR-denominated markets. At 7.8-7.9 CNY/EUR, TLD's China-manufactured GSE is approximately 3-5% more competitive in EUR terms compared to 12 months ago.
GSE demand impact
Improved export competitiveness could boost TLD's market share in Europe and other regions where it competes against European manufacturers. This supports GSE demand from TLD's export markets.
Recommended action
CFO should consider hedging a portion of EUR-denominated export receivables to lock in current favorable rates. Sales team should update pricing for European customers to reflect improved competitiveness.
